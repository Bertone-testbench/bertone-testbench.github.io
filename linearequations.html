A linear equation is a math statement that says two things are equal ‚Äî and it always has a variable (like x) that we're trying to find. Solving it means figuring out what number x must be to make the equation true.

Think of an equation like a perfectly balanced scale. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other ‚Äî or it tips over.

Whatever operation you apply to one side of the equation, apply the same operation to the other side. This keeps the equation balanced and the value of x correct.
